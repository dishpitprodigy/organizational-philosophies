# TDA 70-20-10 Development Plan

This template translates development needs into work. The ratio is not accounting. It is a design principle:

- 70 percent: real work, stretch assignments, shadowing, projects, process improvement, ownership reps, and other experience-based learning.
- 20 percent: coaching, mentoring, peer feedback, communities of practice, reviews, and learning from people who already perform the capability well.
- 10 percent: formal training, courses, reading, labs, certifications, reference material, or structured study.

The important rule is this: training is not evidence of readiness. Training supports readiness. Evidence comes from observed behavior under realistic conditions.

## Use

1. Start from a Personal Development Plan or Competency Calibration.
2. Choose one to three development objectives.
3. Define real work reps first.
4. Add feedback sources.
5. Add formal support.
6. Define risk boundaries and evidence.
7. Review progress at least quarterly.

## 70 Percent: Learning Through Real Work

Choose work that creates legitimate reps without hiding risk or turning development into unsupervised production work.

Possible real-work reps:

- complete a bounded stretch assignment
- shadow a higher-risk workflow, then own a read-only step
- own a reversible change under review
- join a project team with clear scope
- lead a small process improvement
- document a recurring problem, then improve the workflow
- teach another person a task to test understanding
- present a technical or operational finding to the team
- support another team for a defined time box
- run a post-incident improvement item
- build a runbook, checklist, test, automation, or standard work artifact

## 20 Percent: Learning From Others

Feedback should exist before, during, and after the rep. Do not wait until the end to discover that the person practiced the wrong capability.

Possible feedback sources:

- manager coaching
- mentor or coach input
- peer review
- work owner review
- incident review
- design review
- code, document, ticket, or runbook review
- community of practice discussion
- customer, stakeholder, or receiving-team feedback
- shadowing debrief

## 10 Percent: Formal Support

Formal learning should prepare the person for a rep or help them make sense of a rep they already attempted.

Possible formal support:

- course
- lab
- certification path
- reading
- vendor or platform documentation
- internal guide
- recorded session
- workshop
- conference session
- reference implementation

## Progress Check

Use these questions at each checkpoint.

What is different about the person's work now compared with the last review?

What evidence shows improvement?

What evidence shows the gap remains?

What barriers appeared?

What support was missing?

Did the assignment create the intended rep?

Was the risk boundary correct?

Does the plan need to change?

What is the next safe rep?
